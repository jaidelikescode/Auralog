About Auralog

Music has always said things people struggle to put into words.

Auralog is a mood and music tracking app designed to help people understand themselves a little better through the songs they already love. It’s part journal, part reflection tool, and part emotional soundtrack.

Why I Created It

I wanted to create something that felt personal, useful, and a little bit beautiful. A lot of people already use music to process sadness, hype themselves up, sit in nostalgia, calm down, or just survive a weird day. I thought... what if there was a way to actually track that?

Not just what you listened to, but how it connected to your mood, your habits, your memories, and your emotional patterns over time.

What Auralog Is

Auralog is a space where music and self-reflection meet. It helps users log how they feel, connect those feelings to what they’re listening to, and notice patterns they may not have picked up on otherwise.

In other words: it turns your listening history into something a little more meaningful.

The Mission

To help people understand the soundtrack of their lives.

Auralog is built around the idea that music is more than background noise. It can reflect identity, routine, grief, joy, healing, growth, and everything in between. This app is here to make those patterns easier to see.

Reflection

The app encourages users to pause and notice how they feel instead of rushing past it.

Emotion

Auralog treats emotions like real data points, not something random or unimportant.

Creativity

Music is personal, expressive, and deeply tied to memory. The app is built to honor that.

Insight

The goal is not just to collect information, but to help users actually learn something about themselves.

Who It’s For

For people who feel everything through music.

Auralog is for the person with a playlist for every mood, every memory, every almost-relationship, every healing era, every crash out, and every quiet little comeback.

It’s for people who want more than just a streaming app. It’s for people who want context, connection, and a better understanding of themselves.

Your life already has a soundtrack.

Auralog just helps you listen a little closer.
